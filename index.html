Crop Guide & Knowledge Base

Learn about common crops and their diseases, prevention strategies, and best practices.

🌽 Maize

Common Issues:

  • Leaf Spots: Fungal infection common during wet seasons. Improve drainage and spacing.
  • Yellowing: Can indicate nitrogen deficiency. Consider soil testing.
  • Wilting: Check soil moisture and pest damage (armyworms).

Prevention Tips:

  • Use certified disease-resistant varieties
  • Practice crop rotation (3-year cycle)
  • Maintain proper plant spacing for airflow
  • Scout fields weekly during growing season

🍅 Tomato

Common Issues:

  • Early Blight: Brown spots with concentric rings. Remove infected leaves.
  • Powdery Mildew: White coating on leaves. Ensure good air circulation.
  • Blossom End Rot: Dark spots on fruit. Related to calcium and water management.

Prevention Tips:

  • Stake and prune for better airflow
  • Water at soil level, avoid wetting leaves
  • Remove lower leaves as plant grows
  • Use mulch to maintain consistent soil moisture

🥔 Cassava

Common Issues:

  • Cassava Mosaic Disease: Mottled leaves, stunted growth. Use disease-free cuttings.
  • Leaf Spots: Fungal disease in humid climates. Reduce humidity where possible.
  • Root Rot: Poor drainage and waterlogging cause this. Improve field drainage.

Prevention Tips:

  • Always use certified disease-free planting material
  • Practice intercropping to reduce pest populations
  • Ensure good drainage to prevent root diseases
  • Monitor for mosaic disease symptoms early

Best Practices & Expert Tips

Sustainable farming strategies and proven techniques to maximize crop health and yield.

🌱 Soil Health Management

  • Soil Testing: Test soil every 2-3 years to understand nutrient levels and pH
  • Organic Matter: Add compost or manure to improve soil structure and water retention
  • Crop Rotation: Rotate crops to break pest cycles and replenish different nutrients
  • Mulching: Retain moisture, regulate temperature, and suppress weeds

💧 Water Management

  • Irrigation Timing: Water early morning or evening to reduce evaporation
  • Soil Moisture: Check soil moisture before watering (not just surface)
  • Drainage: Ensure proper drainage to prevent waterlogging and root diseases
  • Rainwater Harvesting: Capture rainwater to reduce dependency on other sources

🐛 Integrated Pest Management (IPM)

  • Monitor Regularly: Scout fields 2-3 times per week for early pest detection
  • Biological Control: Encourage natural predators and beneficial insects
  • Cultural Practices: Remove affected leaves, maintain field sanitation
  • Chemical Control: Use pesticides only when necessary and follow safety guidelines

📅 Seasonal Calendar

  • Pre-Season: Prepare soil, test nutrients, select varieties
  • Planting: Choose right timing based on rainfall patterns and temperature
  • Growing Season: Regular monitoring, timely weeding, pest/disease management
  • Harvest: Harvest at optimal maturity for best quality
  • Post-Harvest: Proper storage and handling to reduce losses

About FarmHelp AI

FarmHelp AI is an educational prototype designed to help farmers and students quickly identify crop issues and understand next steps for management.

How It Works

  1. Select your crop type and affected plant part
  2. Describe the visible symptoms
  3. Our AI analyzes the information against our knowledge base
  4. Receive educational guidance on possible issues and inspection steps
  5. Learn recommended management practices

Important Disclaimer

This system does not perform medical diagnosis on crops. It provides educational guidance only. For critical decisions affecting your farm, please consult with:

  • Local agricultural extension officers
  • Qualified agronomists
  • Government agricultural services

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can FarmHelp diagnose diseases?

A: No. FarmHelp provides educational guidance based on symptoms you describe. It helps you understand what to look for and next steps, but a qualified agronomist should make final diagnoses.

Q: What crops does FarmHelp support?

A: Currently, we support Maize, Tomato, and Cassava, with "Other" as a prototype option. We're expanding the knowledge base regularly.

Q: How accurate is the AI guidance?

A: The AI provides educational summaries based on your symptom descriptions. Accuracy depends on accurate symptom reporting and your local conditions. Always verify with local experts.

Q: Can I use this for commercial farming decisions?

A: FarmHelp is designed as an educational tool and prototype. For commercial operations, please consult professional agricultural services.

Q: What data does FarmHelp collect?

A: Your data stays in your browser. We don't collect or store any personal or farm information. Complete privacy protection.

Q: How can I provide feedback or suggest improvements?

A: This is a coursework prototype. Feedback should be directed to the agricultural extension services working with your institution.
